Understanding BMI & BMR

BMI Scale
Metabolism
Health

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight to screen for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

Formula: BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)^2

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it doesn't directly measure body fat or account for muscle mass. Learn more from CDC.

BMI is just one indicator - athletes may have high BMI due to muscle, not fat.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to maintain basic functions at complete rest - like breathing and circulation.

It accounts for 60-75% of daily calorie expenditure! Understanding metabolism.

Factors affecting BMR:

  • Age (decreases with age)
  • Gender (men typically have higher BMR)
  • Body composition (muscle burns more than fat)
  • Genetics and hormones
Building muscle can increase your BMR over time!
